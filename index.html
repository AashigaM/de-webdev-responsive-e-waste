2. Why the "Right to Repair" Matters

What you are seeing: This section uses Flexbox combined with a @media (max-width: 768px) query. On wide screens, the motherboard image and text sit side-by-side. Once the viewport screen gets smaller than 768px, the media query changes the flex direction to stack them vertically for mobile devices.

Photo by Alexandre Debiève on Unsplash

Making Gadgets Last Longer

A lot of tech companies design products that are super hard to fix yourself, forcing us to buy new models every couple of years. The Right to Repair movement is pushing for companies to sell spare parts and share repair manuals with ordinary people.

If we can fix our own hardware or upgrade individual parts, we keep toxic materials out of the dirt and save money at the same time.

3. Small Things We Can Do

Fluid Media
What you are seeing: This layout demonstrates Fluid Media. Both photos use max-width: 100% and height: auto so they shrink down smoothly on smaller phone screens without overflowing past the screen edges.

Drop Off Old Tech Safely

Don't toss dead batteries or old phones in standard trash cans. Check for local e-waste recycling bins at city centers or tech stores.

Photo by Kari Shea on Unsplash

Pass It Along

If you have an old laptop or tablet that still turns on, consider wiping the hard drive and donating it to a local school or student group.
